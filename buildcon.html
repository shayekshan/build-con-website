Also known as plasterboards or drywall, gypsum plasterboards are prefabricated, ready-to-install calcium sulfate boards used for false ceilings. These come in different thicknesses and sizes, such as 9, 12, and 15 mm, and 4 x 6 feet, 4 x 8 feet, and 4 x 10 feet, respectively.Gypsum can serve as a soil amendment, providing a source of calcium, sulfur, and plant nutrients to your gardens or crops. It can also loosens dense soil structures like heavy clay soils and sandy soils by binding small clay particles in a process called flocculation to improve soil aeration and water movement.

One of the many advantages of using it for ceiling design is its ability to provide fire protection. It is completely heat resistant and doesn't embrace temperature either from the atmospheric heat or electricity wires within the house. The most cost-effective, sturdy, and durable false ceiling material, gypsum false ceilings are made from gypsum plasterboards that are affixed to metal sheets with screws. These boards are factory-manufactured and ready-to-install. Since they come in large sheets, they are easier to install and leave no dust in the process. Gypsum ceilings are known for their hydrophobic properties and hence make it a suitable choice for kitchens and bathrooms, whereas its non-combustible core makes it a good choice for commercial spaces.
